Products

The Kamal: The Kamal is a tool used for celestial navigation. Mainly measuring latitude and has been the origin of latitude sailing, first used by Arab navigators. The kamal consists of a rectangular wooden board to which a string with several equally spaced knots is attached through a hole in the middle of the board. It is used by placing one end of the string in your teeth while the other end is held away from the body parallel to the ground. The board is then moved along the string, positioned so the lower edge is even with the horizon, and the upper edge is close to a target star. The angle is then be measured by counting the number of knots from the teeth to the card, or a particular knot can be tied into the string if you are travelling to a known latitude.

Lateen Sails: First introduced by the Romans and brought into Indian, Byzantine, and Arab lands, lateen sails are large, triangular sails which are mounted at an anlge at the mast of a ship. This ship is especially popular due to its ability to travel against the wind resulting in a faster journey and quicker travel time.

The Astrolabe: Although invented by the Greeks, the Astrolabe has been improved and nearly perfected by the Arabs. Used to measure, the position of a astronomical object, this tool can identify stars and planets thus being able to to determine local latitude given local time and vice versa. Astrolabes can help with finding the time of sunrise and the rising of fixed stars in additon to latitude, being much help to sailors, traders, and merchants. It had even been used to schedule important events such as prayers with its amazing accuracy.
